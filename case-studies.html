Case Studies

Demonstrating the framework in practice.

Case studies will apply the Berkeley Budget Discipline Framework to real and hypothetical budget decisions — showing how the six questions change the conversation.

What Each Case Study Will Cover

Every case study follows the same structure, so readers can compare across programs and time periods.

Background

Context about the program, its history, and the budget decisions involved.

Relevant Framework Questions

Which of the six tests apply most directly, and how.

Tradeoffs

What was gained, what was displaced, and what the real choices were.

Evidence

What the data shows about outcomes, costs, and effectiveness.

Alternative Approaches

What other options were available and why they weren't chosen.

Lessons

What the case study teaches about applying the framework to future decisions.

Case studies include both successful and unsuccessful examples. They are not used as personal attacks on individuals or organizations. The goal is institutional learning, not blame.

In the meantime, read the framework and endorse the principles.
